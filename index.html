What is the Quarters format?

Quarters is a budget variant of the MTG commander format.
The format was originally made in the US by the creators of mtgquarters.com.
This is the EU version of the format, since cardmarket.com is the main price indicator here.

In quarters, all cards in the 99 have a price trend of €0.25 or less according to cardmarket.com.
Your commander has a price trend of €1.00 or less.
Of course, all basic lands - including wastes - are legal.

The legal list is recalculated one week after the official release of each main set

FAQ

  • How many cards are legal in quarters?
    Despite the price restriction, roughly 19.300 cards out of 29.000 total EDH-legal cards are legal in quarters for the 99.
    About 1600 cards are legal as a commander, out of roughly 2300 total possible commanders in EDH.

  • When do cards rotate in/out?
    One week after the official release of each main set.
    At that time, we download the price and product lists from Cardmarket that contains all of the cards with their versions/reprints and their price trend.

  • When is a card legal in quarters?
    When a card has at least one version that has a price trend less than or equal to €0.25, the card (and all of its versions) is legal in quarters.
    When at least one version of an EDH-legal commander card is less than or equal to €1.00, it is legal as a commander.
    Of course, only cards that are already legal in the regular commander format can be legal in quarters.
    However, the price trend of an item on Cardmarket can sometimes be bugged. This means that the full details are a bit more complicated.
    Click here for the full story on when a card is legal.

  • Can I play an expensive print of a quarters-legal card?
    Yes, all versions of a card are legal as long as one version of the card is legal.

  • Why play quarters instead of just regular commander?
    Quarters is certainly not meant as a replacement for commander, but as another choice you can make with your friends.
    Opinion: I think the price restriction has some benefits.
    • The format is very budget friendly, since each deck basically costs €25.75 or less.
    • It keeps the price level at a table very similar. You don't have to worry about someone in your pod suddenly rocking up with a 200 dollar card.
    • Making a thematic or funny deck whose primary focus isn't on being good is viable, since you don't have to spend more than €25 on it.
    • You might already have a lot of the cards you need for a deck in your bulk.
